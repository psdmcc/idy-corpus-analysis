Introduction to World Yoga Day. World Yoga Day also known as International Day of Yoga is celebrated every year on June 21. The day was officially declared by the United Nations in December 2014 following a proposal by Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi. The first celebration took place in 2015 and it has since become a global event promoting the physical, mental and spiritual benefits of yoga. Why June 21? June 21 is the summer solstice, the longest day of the year in northern hemisphere. In yogi tradition, this day holds special significance as it is believed to be the day when Adi Yogi, the first yogi, began sharing his knowledge of yoga with humanity. The 11th International Yoga Day on June 21, 2025 aims to highlight unity in health, both human and planetary, as its core message. Whether you are practicing at home, joining a group event or taking part in community initiatives, the idea is to connect your yoga with a broader context of caring our planet. Theme 2025. The official theme for International Yoga Day 2025 is yoga for one earth, one health. This theme highlights the interconnectedness between human health and the health of the planet. It emphasizes that taking care of our environment directly supports our own well-being and vice versa. The message encourages individuals and communities to embrace yoga not only for personal wellness but also as a practice that nurtures collective and ecological harmony. Why this theme? Here is why this theme was chosen. Growing environmental concerns, climate change, pollution, deforestation, and loss of biodiversity are affecting the planet's health and in turn human health. The theme links yoga with ecological consciousness, encouraging people to live in harmony with nature. Post-pandemic awareness. After the coid9 pandemic, there is a stronger global realization of the interconnectedness between human health and planetary health. Clean air, clean water, and a healthy environment are no longer luxuries. They are essentials for survival. Sustainable living through yoga. Yoga promotes values like discipline, simplicity, inner peace and balance which align with the principles of sustainable living. The theme suggests that a yogic lifestyle naturally leads to less consumption, more mindfulness and a lower ecological footprint. Global unity and shared responsibility. The phrase one earth, one health underscores our shared responsibility. Just as yoga connects body, mind and soul, this theme calls for a unified approach connecting people, communities and nations to work together for the well-being of all. It positions yoga as more than a physical exercise, a philosophy and practice that fosters inner and outer balance. Call to action. It invites people to practice yoga not just on the mat but in their choices how they eat, travel, consume and interact with the world. In essence, yoga for one earth, one health reflects the evolving role of yoga in the 21st century. A tool not just for personal transformation but also for planetary healing and collective harmony.
