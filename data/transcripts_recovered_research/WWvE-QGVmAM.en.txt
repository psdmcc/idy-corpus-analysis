[Music] As the sun rises over the mountains, millions begin their day with yoga. The practice rooted in India over 5,000 years ago began as a spiritual discipline uniting the mind, body and soul. Ancient texts like the yog sutras of Patanjali laid its foundation emphasizing meditation and physical postures. In the late 19th and early 20th centuries, pioneers like Swami Viveananda introduced yoga to the west, captivating audiences with its philosophy of balance and inner peace. Fast forward to the 21st century, yoga has exploded globally, fueled by its adaptability. From hater to vinyas, yoga's physical benefits like stress relief and improved flexibility have drawn millions. By 2025, over 300 million people worldwide practice yoga with studios in every major city. Social media and celebrity endorsements have amplified its appeal, while scientific studies have validated its benefits for mental health and physical fitness. India proposed the international day of yoga which was unanimously adopted by the United Nations. Since then, millions gather annually on June 21st from New York to Nairobi celebrating yoga's unifying power. Events like mass yoga sessions and virtual classes highlight its role in promoting wellness and peace in a fast-paced world. This ancient practice born in India has stretched its way into hearts and homes across the globe. From serene studios to bustling city parks, yoga's popularity continues to soar. For centuries, yoga has been a cherished practice. Harmonizing mind, body, and soul. It is a powerful path to physical health and inner calm. It has been adopted by millions across the globe.
