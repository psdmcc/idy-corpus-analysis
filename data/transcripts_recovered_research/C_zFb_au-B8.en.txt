Once upon a time thousands of years ago in ancient India, people had the same problem you have right now. Their minds wouldn't stop chattering. So they sat down and asked, "What if there was a way to find peace inside this chaos?" Legend says the first yogi, Adi Yogi, the Lord Shiva, discovered something magical. He realized that by simply sitting still and breathing deeply, he could quiet the storm in his head. He became like a calm lake. No ripples, just perfect reflection. Wait, wait. What if I could teach this to everyone? So these ancient seekers became students of nature. They watched eagles focus, trees stand strong, and rivers flow gracefully. Let's copy them, they said, creating poses called assan. Then came their biggest discovery, breath. They noticed when you're stressed, you breathe fast. When you're calm, you breathe slow. What if we control our breath to control our feelings? For thousands of years, this beautiful practice called yoga traveled from teacher to student like passing a torch. Then something amazing happened. The whole world got busy. Phones buzzing, minds racing, everyone feeling stressed. We need that ancient peace, people realized. So yoga traveled from India to every corner of the world. Now millions practice together. From Tokyo to New York, from beaches to bedrooms. It's India's greatest gift to humanity. A simple reminder that peace isn't somewhere out there. It's right here, one breath away. Happy International Yoga Day. Your journey to inner calm starts now.
