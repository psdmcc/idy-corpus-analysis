The inaugural session of a yoga connect a global yoga summit was organized in New Delhi by the ministry of Aayush. This was an opportunity to bring together people who are shaping the future of yoga. Yoga gurus, policy makers, health experts, business leaders, researchers and global influencers both from India and abroad attended the summit. A major highlight of the summit was the release of yoga prabhava report a nationwide study conducted by the central council for research in yoga and nitropathy assessing the impact of international day of yoga over the past decade. A large gathering assembled at the historic Krishna temple in Nepal's uh patan darbar a UNESCO heritage site for a yoga session ahead of the international day of yoga on June 21st. Enthusiastic participants including children and women, elderly and the youth followed guidance from a yogu during the event. The session organized by the embassy of India is aimed to raise awareness on the theme yoga for one earth one health. Notable attendees included Lalithpur's deputy mayor, Nepali film actors Harib Bancha and Nikl upriti who joined the practice and encouraged people to embrace yoga in their daily lives.
