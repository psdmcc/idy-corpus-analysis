Introduction
The United Nations (UN) 17 Sustainable 
Development Goals (SDGs) aim toward 
promoting community and environmental 
sustainability.[1] The environment of a 
human being often can have an impact 
on their mental health and well-being. 
Scientific studies have well established 
that exposure to nature is positively 
associated with numerous aspects of both 
physiological and psychological health, 
including positive impacts on anxiety, 
ADHD, aggression, depression, PTSD, 
and stress. [2-4] This synergistic relationship 
between a human being and their natural 
environment is an important aspect of 
environmental sustainability. Thus, methods 
of promoting natural lifestyles that are 
conducive to environmental sustainability 
need to be explored. [5]

minimalistic life with contentment that

Abstract
Context: The SDGs highlight the connections between the environmental and socioeconomic 
aspects of sustainable development. Yoga not only results in one’s physical and mental resilience 
but also develops positive feelings toward one’s environment to help protect it from destruction. 
Aims: The study aimed to understand the role of Yoga for achieving the United Nations SDGs and 
to draft a policy statement for its implementation to achieve the SDGs. Methodology: The study 
was conducted in three phases. Phase 1 included a review of available literature. Phase 2 included 
contacting experts from Yoga, community health, social work, and medicine from all over India and 
across the globe for discussion. Phase 3 included drafting the recommendations to be submitted 
to the CCRYN based on the results from the first two phases. Results: A total of 320 research 
articles (217 included) were screened. A  total of 103 were excluded for reasons (being not relevant, 
inability to get full text of articles, and outcome of the study not matching). Phase 2 was conducted 
in three sessions with different groups of experts for their input. In phase 3, recommendations were 
finalized, and the policy statement was drafted. Conclusions: Yoga can help in achieving the targets 
of the UN SDGs. The recommendations, if implemented, can solve the issues endangering nature 
and the ecosystem. Thus, Yoga builds a harmony between humans and nature.

Methodology
It was an exploratory study design and, therefore, did not 
include any sample size, intervention, and data analysis. 
The study was conducted in three phases: Phase 1 included 
review of literature, Phase 2 included brainstorming with 
experts, and Phase 3 included recommendations, policy 
drafting, and report submission to CCRYN.
Phase 1: Review of literature
The process of review of literature in Phase 1, included 
a search of “ Yoga” related studies in each SDG (The 
keywords were prepared for each sub-target of all the 
seventeen SDGs using Yoga as an intervention, for example, 
Yoga for eradicating poverty/zero hunger/gender equality/
climate action/education, etc.) using databases PubMed, 
Scopus, Cochrane Library, and Google Scholar between 
July 2023 and September 2023. Articles were screened 
after going through the title, abstract, and the complete 
paper. Relevant data from the articles were saved in the 
Microsoft Excel sheet (Microsoft corporation, Redmond, 
Washington, United States of America).
Phase 2: Brainstorming with the experts
Three brainstorming sessions were conducted with 
the experts, (a) In-Person stakeholder’s workshop, (b) 
Presentation and discussion with the members of the Indian 
Yoga Association, and (c) Online stakeholder’s discussion 
across different countries.
In-person stakeholder’ s workshop
A brainstorming discussion was done with twenty experts from 
different schools of Yoga, such as ( Patanjali Yoga University, 
Haridwar; Krishnamacharya Yoga Mandir, Chennai; 
Kaivalyadhama, Pune; Iyengar School of Yoga, Mumbai; 
and SVYASA Yoga University, Bengaluru). Apart from Yoga 
experts, other experts involved were from different fields 
such as medicine, social work, environment, naturopathy, and 
ayurveda. It helped us to understand how Yoga can be used to 
achieve the UN SDGs and to prepare a white paper.
Presentation of “Yoga for achieving UN SDGs” at 
National Conclave organized by Indian Yoga Association in 
collaboration with Art of Living Headquarters, Bengaluru
An in-person presentation was done on the draft of 
recommendations generated from phase 1 in front of the 
members of the Indian Yoga Association (IYA) during a 
National Conclave at Art of Living Ashram, Bengaluru, and 
discussed how IYA can participate to achieve them. The 
number of delegates, apart from the members of IYA who 
attended the conclave, was more than 100 from various 
backgrounds such as Yoga, social work, educationists, 
environmentalists, and researchers.
Online stakeholders discussion
An online stakeholders’ discussion was conducted on 
Yoga for achieving UN SDGs with ten experts from 
Yoga schools and associations from different countries. 
Experts were from The United States of America, Bahrain, 
Portugal, Italy, Japan, South Korea, Singapore, Germany, 
Brazil, and Vietnam. The experts were presented with the 
draft recommendations and were asked for their inputs/
suggestions/modifications, if any, for a particular SDG.
Phase 3: Policy drafting (recommendations)
A draft on recommendations was prepared and circulated 
among all the experts involved in phase 2 to ask 
for their inputs/comments/consent for finalizing the 
recommendations before submission to the Central Council 
for Research in Yoga and Naturopathy (CCRYN) under the 
Ministry of AYUSH, Government of India.
Results
Phase 1: Review of literature
The articles were screened by two separate experts (AJ and 
VK), and the final decision about the inclusion/exclusion 
of the articles was made. Reasons for excluding the 
articles were: Does not directly talk about the related topic, 
inability to get full text of articles, outcome of the study 
not matching, not related to Yoga, etc. None of the articles 
referred to more than one SDG. The results of the search 
are given in Table 1.
Overall, the review revealed the role of Yoga in various 
SDGs as explained further. For SDG 3, we got the 
maximum relevant studies. Yoga has a strong potential as 
an intervention for the maintenance of health and curing 
ailments. Yoga is used in education for ethical learning in 
SDG 4 and SDG 13; Yoga as a source of earning in SDG 1, 
SDG 2, and SDG 8; Yoga as a cultural contribution in SDG 
5 and SDG 8; Yoga as a tool to spread peace, harmony in 
nature and health across the community and globe in SDG 
13, SDG 15, SDG 16 and SDG 17.

Phase 3: Policy drafting (recommendations):
A policy statement was drafted as “Recommendations

Discussion
Based on the recommendations on how Yoga can help 
achieve the UN SDGs, a few achievable outcomes were 
suggested for implementation:
Yoga is a way of life: All through the discussion one 
point that has been prominent is that Yoga is a way of 
life (following Yoga philosophy and Yogic practices), 
which can help in achieving the UN SDGs. Hence, 
beyond Yoga practices of asana, pranayama, and 
meditation, it is important to look at Yoga philosophy 
and concepts to help achieve the UN SDGs. Several 
concepts from Yoga philosophy can be indirectly applied 
to all the SDGs, for example, swadhyaya (self-reflection), 
mindfulness (Awareness of the present moment), 
trigunas (three qualities in every individual), 
tapas (self-discipline). However, in this document, we 
have only mentioned the Yoga philosophy concepts that 
can directly help achieve the specific UN SDG. The 
recommendations need to be read and interpreted in 
the above context. The concepts of Yoga go beyond the 
ideas of the SDGs by the UN, which gets revised every 
10 years. Yogic way of life remains relevant beyond the 
targets and time frame of the UN SDGs.
1. Incorporating  recommendations  in existing projects: 
The recommendations in this document can be 
incorporated into the existing campaigns and 
programs of the UN. For example, several UN 
agencies have projects on reducing food wastage with 
circular economy principles, projects for women, 
resilience of vulnerable individuals, etc., where 
these recommendations on Yoga and Yoga practices 
to achieve the specific SDGs can be incorporated. 
These recommendations can be linked to tangible 
outcomes that can help countries achieve the SDGs 
through Yoga. The current team would be happy to 
volunteer to review all UN projects related to each of 
the SDGs and provide specific guidance on how these 
recommendations can be woven into the existing 
projects.
2. Collaborative research for implementation of 
recommendations: Efficacy of Yoga has already been 
validated in several thrust areas of the UN, such as 
prevention of violence, public health systems, etc. Over 
a period, some of these SDG-specific recommendations 
can be developed into new projects/programs for testing 
and implementation across countries.
3. Convene discussions on Yoga for SDGs: The UN can 
convene discussions and round tables on Yoga for 
achieving SDGs as a lead-up to a Joint UN conference 
on Yoga for achieving SDGs for International Yoga 
Day 2025. These discussions would help finalize the 
way forward in research and implementation of the 
recommendations across countries.
Conclusion
Yoga has an important role in achieving the UN SDGs. 
The recommendations, if implemented nationwide, help 
solve the issues endangering nature and the ecosystem, thus 
building a harmony between humans and nature.
